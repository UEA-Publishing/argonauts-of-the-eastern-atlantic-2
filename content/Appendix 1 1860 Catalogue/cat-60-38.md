AFRICAN MISSION. Red.


173 **Hat** from the interior of South Africa.—Presented by Rev.
J. J. Freeman.

174 Model of African wagon and plough.—Presented by Rev. J.
J. Freeman. Wagon made by a native artizan.

***MADAGASCAR.***

171, 172 **Coarse thick cotton shawl** or searf, and a striped silk
ditto.

173 **Cloth made of rofia**, a species of palm, worn by the men in
Betsiamisaraka.

174 **Ditto**, made of dyed rofia. From Betanimena.

175 **Coarse cloth**, made of rofia.

176, 177 **Two very fine rofia shawls**.

178—180 **Various specimens of cotton** and rofia cloth.

181 **Square fans of matting**, tastily ornamented, with long
handles.

182—184 **Two flag-shaped fans**, and two others, much coarser.

185, 186 A deep ladle, and two spoons, made of horn.

187 **Two snuff-boxes**, made of portions of horns.

188 **A powder-horn**, usually worn merely às an ornament.

189 **Two pointed instruments of bone**.

190 **A brush**, made of a curious kind of grass.

191 **A sword and scabbard**, made by natives of Madagascar in
imitation of the Chinese.

192—198 **Three hatchets**, a spade, planes, chisels, ladles, &c.,
made by the natives.

199—204 **Various knives**; one has the handle made of horn, in¬
laid with brass.

205 **Pair of shoes** made of Madagascar leather.

206 **Seeds of the rofia palm**.
