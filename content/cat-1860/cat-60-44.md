In the horizontal Case U.
MISCELLANEOUS ARTICLES, UNCONNECTED WITH
THE MISSIONS.
1 Cast of a medallion of Pope Gregory XVI.
2—4 A crucifix, and two other Roman Catholic subjects, en-
graved on mother of pearl, at Bethlehem.—Presented by
Rev. Mr. Lowndes.
5—7 Three large mother of pearl shells, well engraved, brought
from Alexandria.—Presented by Rev. William Thompson.
8, 9 Two circular plates of wood, with inscriptions painted on
them in very old orthography.
10, 11 Two rosaries with crucifixes.
12 Feather flowers, made by the nuns at Malta.
13 A miniature copy of the Koran, written in Arabic.—Presented
by the late D. Dale, Esq., Judge of Moorshedabad; sent
by Rev. M. Hill.
14 Another, on small round pieces of paper, attached side by
side, sometimes worn by Mussulmans in a kind of locket
suspended from the neck ; this was also presented by Mr.
Dale.
15 Chinese coins attached to a small tortoise shell, and a piece
of silk.
Other coins of the following emperors:—
16 KANG-HI, who ruled from 1661 to 1722.
17 YANGSHIN, 1722 to 1735. KIEN-LUNG, 1735 to 1795.
18 KEA KING, 1795 to 1826, and one probably of the
19 MIng dynasty, which lasted from 1368 to 1649.
20 A coin of Schah ALEM II., 1758, sovereign of Delhi.
21 Two other oriental coins.
