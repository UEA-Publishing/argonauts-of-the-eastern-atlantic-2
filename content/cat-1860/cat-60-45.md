MISCELLANEOUS ARTICLES.
White.)
22, 23 Two small sepulchral figures, with perpendicular lines of
hieroglyphics down the front of each; taken from the
coffins of Egyptian mummies.—Presented by Rev William
Thompson.
24 Specimens of mummy cloth.
25 A Roman lamp of terra cotta.
26 An Unguentarium of ditto, usually called lachrymatories, or
tear vessels. Athens.
From Herculaneum.
27 Fragments of tesselated pavement.
28 An ancient silver coin of Corinth.
29 Another, of Alexander the Great.
30 A bronze medal of Ptolemy of Egypt.
31 A beautiful set of Jubilee Medals.—Presented by Mr. J. Davis.
Retvna 1687
