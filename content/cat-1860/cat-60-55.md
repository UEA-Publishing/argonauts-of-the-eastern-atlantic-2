NATURAL HISTORY.
White.)
On the other side
Are two rich blocks of Osseous Breccia and two gigantic fossil
oysters from Cerigo.—Presented by Rev. I. Lowndes. Also,
various vegetable productions, including the Phormium
tenax, or New Zealand flax, leaves of the Palmyra from
Travancore, seeds of the castor-oil plant, Ricinus commu-
nis, ditto of Coix lacryma, or Job’s tears, camel-thorn, the
thorn, in Africa called “stop awhile,” &c., &c.
Case Z in the Lobby
Contains the larger vegetable bodies, viz., two of the double, or
sea cocoa-nut, Lodoicea Sechellarium, base of the fronds
of a Polypodium, or Scythian lamb, embryo leaf of a palm,
various calabashes and gourds, cotton pods, seeds of
acacia, specimens of wood, the Bread fruit, &c., &c.
Two frames, containing twelve drawings of African flowers.-
Presented by Dr. Thom.
THE END.
London: Reed and Pardon, Printers, Paternoster-row.
