NATURAL HISTORY.
9 The BoSH-Bok, A. sylvatica.
10 The GEMs-BoK, A. oryx, Pallas.
11 The TaHarrsI of the Bechuanas, and RoAN ANTELoe of the
Colonists, A. leucophæa, Gm.
12, 13 The CANNA, A. oreas, Pallas.
14 The Cape BUFFALo, Bos caffra, of large size : the flesh of
this animal served as food for Mr. Campbell’s party
several days.
Case P, on the top of the large Cases, contains
Fifteen various birds, including the Crow and the King Birds of
Paradise, two large Indian Kingfishers, Parrot, Hawk, &c.
Case Q.
BoA CONSTRICTOR in the act of destroying a fawn, from the neigh-
bourhood of Demerara.—Presented by Rev. Mr. Wray.
Case R.
A Mangouste, Ratel, earless Marmot, an Iguana, Monitor
lizard, and heads of two species of Buceros, or Hornbill.
Case S.
The STEIN-BoK, A. rupestris, and the KLIP SPRINGÉR, both from
Africa.—Presented by Rév. J. Campbell.
Case T.
Two examples of the FLYING MACAUCA, Galeopithecus volans, a
small brown Deer, the King Vulture, and four other birds.
