NATURAL HISTORY. White.


41 **Carapace**, or back shell of a large edible Turtle, Chelonia
Midas. From the West Indies.

42—46 **Five small species of Testudines**, or land Tortoises.

47 **A fresh-water turtle**. Emys.

**PISCES.**

48 A stuffed specimen of the** flying gurnard**, Trigla volitans.

49, 50 **Inflated skins of the Diodon**, or porcupine globe fish.

51 **Ditto of the trunk-fish**, Ostracion.

52 **The sea-horse**, Sygnathus hippocampus.

53 **Vertebral column of a shark**, Squalus.

54 **Jaws** of ditto.

55, 56 Two very large specimens of the rostrum of the** saw-fish**,
Pristis antiquorum.

57, 58 **Ditto of another species**, Pristis pectinatus.

59, 60 **Several of a very small species**. From Calcutta.

61, 62 **Tail,** and several of the bony spines of the sting ray,
Trygon.

***Cases I, J, and K.***

**AVES.**

63 The **CASSOWARY**, Casuarius. From India. Like the ostrich, it
is unable to fly, but runs with great swiftness, being aided
by its short wings.

64 **Eggs** of the same.

65 **Ostrich eggs**, one rudely engraved by a Hottentot.

66 **Two eggs**.

67 **The head and wings of a Patagonian Penguin**, Spheniscus.

68 **The eared Penguin.**

69 **Auk.**

69* **A Pelican**, Pelicanus onicrotalus.—Presented by Rev. I.
Lowndes, Corfu.

69** **A male Vulture**.—Presented by Rev. I. Loundes, Corfu.
